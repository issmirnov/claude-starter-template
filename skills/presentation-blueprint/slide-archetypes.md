# Slide Archetypes

Reusable slide patterns that bridge the strategic outline (Phase 3) to HTML rendering (Phase 5). Each archetype defines layout, content requirements, and style variations per presentation type.

When rendering, select the archetype matching each slide's role, then apply the style variation for the active presentation type.

---

## 1. Title

**When to use:** Opening slide of every presentation. Sets tone and context.

**Layout:**
- Headline text centered vertically, occupying ~60% of slide width
- Optional subtitle below headline at ~40% font size
- Optional logo or wordmark in bottom-right or top-left corner
- Large whitespace margins — no clutter

**Content requirements:**
- Headline (required): presentation title or hook phrase, max 8 words
- Subtitle (optional): one-line clarifier — date, company name, tagline
- Logo (optional): image URL or company identifier

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Dark background, bold sans-serif headline, high-contrast accent color on subtitle |
| Technical | Light or white background, clean monospace or sans-serif headline, subtle brand color |
| Update | Neutral background, standard sans-serif, date prominent in subtitle position |

---

## 2. Section Divider

**When to use:** Marks transition between major sections. Gives the audience a mental reset.

**Layout:**
- Section title centered both horizontally and vertically
- Optional section number or icon above the title
- Generous padding — at least 30% margin on all sides
- Optional thin accent line or geometric element

**Content requirements:**
- Section title (required): 2-5 words
- Section number (optional): e.g., "01", "Part 2"

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Bold accent-colored background, white text, full-bleed color |
| Technical | White background, large lightweight text, thin horizontal rule |
| Update | Muted background tint, standard weight text, minimal decoration |

---

## 3. Problem/Pain

**When to use:** Problem definition, pain point articulation, "why this matters" slides. Designed for emotional weight.

**Layout:**
- Primary statement at top third, large bold text
- Supporting evidence in bottom two-thirds: 2-3 data points, short quotes, or bullet fragments
- Optional background image (darkened/blurred) for emotional resonance
- Visual weight skewed toward the statement — evidence is secondary

**Content requirements:**
- Problem statement (required): one sentence, max 12 words
- Supporting evidence (required): 2-3 items — stats, quotes, or concise pain descriptions
- Background image (optional): relevant contextual image

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Dark/dramatic background, red or warm accent for urgency, bold statement text |
| Technical | Clean layout, problem framed as technical constraint or limitation, monochrome palette |
| Update | Neutral tone, problem framed as challenge or blocker, data-backed evidence |

---

## 4. Solution

**When to use:** Product/feature showcase, the "what we built" moment, answer to the problem slide.

**Layout:**
- Headline statement across top (~20% of slide height)
- Hero visual in center: product screenshot, before/after comparison, or demo mockup (~60% of slide)
- 2-3 short benefit callouts below or beside the visual
- Optional arrow or transition element connecting to previous problem slide's theme

**Content requirements:**
- Solution headline (required): one sentence framing the solution, max 10 words
- Hero visual (recommended): screenshot, mockup, or diagram
- Benefit callouts (required): 2-3 phrases, each max 6 words

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Bright/optimistic accent color, product screenshot with subtle shadow/glow, confident tone |
| Technical | Architecture snippet or code sample as hero, clean annotations, neutral palette |
| Update | Feature screenshot with highlight annotations, matter-of-fact tone |

---

## 5. Metric/KPI

**When to use:** Traction slides, performance dashboards, status metrics. One big number with context.

**Layout:**
- Hero number centered, very large (3-5x body text size), occupying ~40% of slide
- Label above or below the number explaining what it measures
- Trend indicator beside the number: arrow, sparkline, or percentage change
- 1-2 context lines at bottom: comparison period, benchmark, or brief explanation

**Content requirements:**
- Metric value (required): the number, formatted appropriately (e.g., "$2.4M", "47%", "10x")
- Metric label (required): what the number represents, max 5 words
- Trend indicator (recommended): direction + magnitude (e.g., "+32% QoQ", up arrow)
- Context line (optional): benchmark or time period

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Oversized number in accent color, dark background, trend arrow in green/red |
| Technical | Monospace number, performance benchmark framing, sparkline if available |
| Update | Dashboard-style layout, multiple metrics allowed (up to 3), neutral color with green/red for trends |

---

## 6. Comparison

**When to use:** Competitive positioning, before/after analysis, feature matrices, option evaluation.

**Layout:**
- Two-column split (equal width) or feature-matrix table
- Column headers clearly labeled (Us vs. Them, Before vs. After, Option A vs. Option B)
- Visual differentiation: winning column highlighted with accent color or checkmarks
- Optional summary row or winner callout at bottom

**Content requirements:**
- Column headers (required): 2 labels identifying each side
- Comparison items (required): 3-6 rows of comparison points
- Advantage indicators (recommended): checkmarks, color coding, or bold for winning attributes
- Summary statement (optional): one-line takeaway

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Bold "us vs. them" framing, accent color on winning column, checkmark/x icons |
| Technical | Feature matrix with detailed specs, neutral tone, shaded alternating rows |
| Update | Before/after framing, progress indicators, data-driven comparison |

---

## 7. Quote/Testimonial

**When to use:** Social proof, user voice, expert endorsement, memorable pull quote.

**Layout:**
- Large quotation marks or quote glyph as decorative element
- Quote text centered, occupying ~50% of slide, larger than body text (1.5-2x)
- Attribution below quote: name, title, company — right-aligned or centered
- Optional headshot or company logo beside attribution
- Generous whitespace — let the words breathe

**Content requirements:**
- Quote text (required): 1-3 sentences, max 40 words
- Attribution name (required): who said it
- Attribution detail (optional): title, company, role
- Headshot or logo (optional): image for credibility

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Oversized decorative quotation marks in accent color, dark background, white italic text |
| Technical | Clean serif or sans-serif quote, minimal decoration, light background |
| Update | Standard formatting, emphasis on the data or result mentioned in the quote |

---

## 8. Team/People

**When to use:** Team introduction, key personnel highlights, organizational overview.

**Layout:**
- Grid of team members: 2-6 people in a row, or 2x3 grid for larger teams
- Each person: name, role/title, optional headshot placeholder (circle or rounded square)
- Optional one-line bio or key credential per person
- Slide title at top: "The Team", "Leadership", or custom

**Content requirements:**
- People list (required): name + role for each person, 2-8 people
- Headshots (optional): image URLs or placeholder circles with initials
- Credentials (optional): one notable achievement or background per person, max 8 words
- Slide title (optional): defaults to "Team"

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Clean grid with circular headshot placeholders, credentials emphasize relevant experience, dark or light background |
| Technical | Minimal grid, emphasis on technical expertise and contributions |
| Update | Compact list format acceptable, role-focused rather than bio-focused |

---

## 9. Diagram/Architecture

**When to use:** System architecture, process flows, technical explanations, workflow visualization.

**Layout:**
- Diagram occupies ~70% of slide center area
- Title/label at top
- Diagram rendered as structured HTML: boxes + arrows, flowchart, or layered architecture
- Optional legend or annotation labels
- Minimal surrounding text — the diagram is the content

**Content requirements:**
- Diagram title (required): what the diagram shows, max 8 words
- Components (required): named boxes/nodes with labels
- Connections (required): arrows or lines showing relationships, with optional labels
- Legend (optional): color or shape meanings

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Simplified, 4-6 components max, accent color for key component, clean arrows |
| Technical | Detailed, more components allowed (up to 10), monospace labels, layered/grouped layout |
| Update | Process-focused, status indicators on components (green/yellow/red), timeline orientation |

---

## 10. Bullet List

**When to use:** Key points, feature lists, process steps. Use sparingly to avoid wall-of-text slides.

**Layout:**
- Slide title at top
- 3-5 bullet points with clear visual hierarchy
- Each bullet: short phrase (max 8 words) with optional one-line supporting detail in smaller text
- Left-aligned text with consistent indentation
- Optional icon or number prefix per bullet

**Content requirements:**
- Slide title (required): what this list covers
- Bullet items (required): 3-5 items, each max 8 words for the headline
- Supporting detail (optional): one line per bullet, smaller text
- Icons or numbers (optional): visual prefix for each item

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Bold bullet headlines, accent-colored icons or numbers, minimal supporting text |
| Technical | Numbered or icon-prefixed, more supporting detail allowed, structured/formal |
| Update | Checkbox-style bullets for status items, green/yellow/red indicators |

---

## 11. Image-Heavy

**When to use:** Emotional impact, product screenshots, real-world context, visual storytelling.

**Layout:**
- Image occupies 80-100% of slide (full-bleed or large centered)
- Optional dark overlay gradient for text readability
- Minimal text overlay: headline or short caption, positioned at bottom or top
- No bullets, no data — the image carries the message

**Content requirements:**
- Image (required): URL or description for generation, high-resolution preferred
- Headline overlay (optional): max 6 words, placed over image with contrast treatment
- Caption (optional): one line below image if not full-bleed

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Full-bleed with dark gradient overlay, white bold text overlay, cinematic feel |
| Technical | Product screenshot with annotation callouts, clean border, light background padding |
| Update | Screenshot with highlighted area of interest, minimal overlay text |

---

## 12. CTA/Ask

**When to use:** Final slide. Call to action, specific request, contact information, next steps.

**Layout:**
- Primary ask/CTA headline centered, large bold text (~40% of slide)
- 2-3 supporting details or next steps below
- Contact information or links at bottom
- Optional logo reprise
- Clean and uncluttered — the ask must be unmistakable

**Content requirements:**
- CTA headline (required): the specific ask, max 8 words (e.g., "Join our $5M seed round")
- Supporting details (optional): 2-3 lines — timeline, terms, next steps
- Contact info (recommended): email, website, or calendar link
- Logo (optional): brand reinforcement

**Style variations:**
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Pitch | Bold accent background, large white CTA text, urgency-driven tone, investment terms prominent |
| Technical | Clean layout, "Get started" or "Try it" framing, documentation/repo links |
| Update | "Next steps" framing, action items with owners, timeline if applicable |

## Usage Notes

- **Archetype selection happens during Phase 3 (Outline).** Each slide in the outline maps to one archetype.
- **Style variation is resolved during Phase 5 (Render).** The presentation type determines which column of the style table applies.
- **Archetypes are composable.** A slide can blend elements (e.g., a Solution slide with a Metric callout), but one archetype should dominate the layout.
- **Content limits are guidelines.** If a slide needs 6 bullets instead of 5, that is fine. If it needs 12, split into two slides.
- **Images are optional unless the archetype says required.** When no image is available, use color blocks, geometric shapes, or icon placeholders.
